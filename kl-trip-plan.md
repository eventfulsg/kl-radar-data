## 1. The mission (why this trip exists)

Eventful SG works because of one loop: **spot what's moving early → cover it fast → in house style → repeat daily.** Malaysia is the same playbook with a bigger map and less competition per capita — the KL Foodie network proves the model prints there.

This trip has 3 jobs, in order:

1. **Relationships** — meet the 6 people below. Partners > followers on day one.
2. **Proof of content** — 8 daily vlogs that double as the founding content for the MY presence. The expansion story IS the content.
3. **Ground truth** — the radar tells us what's trending in KL; the trip tells us *why*. Walk the spots the radar keeps surfacing (Foodie network turf, TSL MY beats) and calibrate.

**Unfair advantage:** the radar already scans 21 KL pages every morning at 8am and ranks by outperformance. Nobody else lands in KL with a machine telling them what's blowing up *today*. Use it daily (see §5).

## 4. Content plan

### The hook series (locked format, one per day)
> **"Day X/8 of expanding my media business into Malaysia"**

Consistent opener, consistent counter, cliffhanger each day into the next. The 8-day countdown structure does the retention work for you.

### Day 1 script (full)
> This is day 1 of expanding my media business into Malaysia — and I'm gonna vlog every single part of it.
>
> Quick context: I run Eventful, a media business in Singapore. We cover food, events, deals — and I've been watching the Malaysian scene for months. Not vibes — actual data. I built a radar that scans 21 Malaysian pages every morning and tells me exactly what's blowing up in KL before I even get out of bed.
>
> So here's the plan. 8 days. Every day: one cultural spot, one local food spot, one business meeting. Cheesemakers, kombucha brewers, a ticketing platform, a heritage kopitiam — the people actually building things here.
>
> By day 8 I'll answer one question: is Eventful expanding into Malaysia — or not?
>
> Day 1 starts now. First stop: [KLCC].

### Daily script skeleton (days 2–7)
1. **Hook (5s):** "Day X/8 of expanding my media business into Malaysia. Today: [one-line objective]."
2. **Checklist recap (5s):** the 3 daily objectives on screen, tick them live through the day.
3. **Cultural spot (30–45s):** one genuine observation, not tourist narration.
4. **Food spot (30–45s):** house hard rule applies to the vlog too — **say a number** (price in RM, queue time, years open).
5. **Meeting (45–60s):** film the arrival + one insight the person said (with permission). Business value spoken plainly: "here's what I learned about how money moves in MY media."
6. **Cliffhanger (5s):** tomorrow's meeting or spot.

### Day 8 script (the payoff)
Recap all 6 meetings in one line each → the honest math (market size, what people said they'd pay for, what the radar shows daily) → the decision. Don't soft-land it; commit to an answer on camera. That video is the one that gets clipped.

### Shot checklist (every day, non-negotiable)
- [ ] Talking-head hook, shot in the morning while fresh
- [ ] Walking-in establishing shot of each of the 3 stops
- [ ] Food close-up + first-bite reaction
- [ ] One "insight to camera" after the meeting (outside, right after — memory is best for 5 minutes)
- [ ] Cliffhanger line before bed
- [ ] B-roll buffer: traffic, LRT, signage, street sounds — 2 min minimum

### Where it posts
- Daily vlog → @eventfulsg (the hook literally tags it) — TikTok/Reels/Shorts.
- **Secure the MY handle before flying** (eventful.my / eventfulmy — whichever is free, lock all platforms). The vlog series becomes its founding content; day 1 of the MY page is day 1 of the expansion. Cross-post from day 1 even at zero followers — the archive is the asset.
- Captions on the MY page follow the house cheat sheet already in the radar app: number in every food caption, emoji info block (🗓️ ⏰ 📍 🚇 🚗), 3–5 hashtags #malaysia #klfoodie + niche.
