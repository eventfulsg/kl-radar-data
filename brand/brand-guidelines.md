## 2. Logo

The Eventful mark is a **location pin** holding a **magnifying glass** with a **smiley face** inside — we search for the fun, find it, and put it on the map. The wordmark is lowercase, rounded, and friendly: **eventful**.

### Lockups
1. **Primary (stacked):** pin mark above the lowercase wordmark. Default for profile images, decks, watermarks.
2. **Horizontal:** mark beside wordmark. Use in headers, footers and co-branding strips.
3. **Mark only:** the pin + smiley on its own. Only at small sizes (favicons, avatars, watermark corners) where the wordmark would be illegible.
4. **Regional lockup:** primary lockup + thin horizontal divider + country name in white uppercase (e.g. **MALAYSIA**). This is the template for every new market. Country name is set in a clean geometric sans, letter-spaced, all caps.

### Color usage
- **Default:** white logo on Eventful Orange (#F26F21).
- **Reversed:** orange logo on white or Cream backgrounds.
- **One-color:** all-white or all-ink (#2B2521) when overlaying photos/video — white over dark footage, ink over light.

### Rules
- Clear space: keep at least the height of the smiley face clear on all sides.
- Minimum size: don't shrink the stacked lockup below ~48px tall on screen; switch to mark-only below that.
- Don't recolor the logo outside the approved palette, stretch it, rotate it, add drop shadows/outlines, or place it on busy areas of a photo.
- Don't redraw the smiley or swap the wordmark font.

## 4. Typography

- **Display / wordmark-adjacent:** a rounded, friendly geometric sans. Closest widely-available matches: **Baloo 2** or **Quicksand (Bold)**. Use for big playful headlines ("let's sell out your next event :)").
- **Headings & UI:** a clean geometric sans — **Poppins** or **Montserrat** (SemiBold/Bold). This matches the caps style used in the regional lockups and deck headers.
- **Body:** **Inter** or system sans, regular weight, generous line height.
- Numbers are part of our voice — make stats big and bold (`>10M`, `>700K`, `800%`). Always keep the `>` where it's true; it reads as receipts, not rounding.
- Lowercase is a stylistic asset for punchlines and the wordmark; use sentence case for information, ALL CAPS only for short labels (MALAYSIA, PRICING) — never for whole sentences (that's the hype-page style we make fun of).

## 5. Photography & Content Style

- **Real over produced.** Phone-shot, in-the-crowd energy. Faces, queues, food close-ups, steam, neon, mess. No sterile stock photography.
- **Static collages:** dense, informative, thumb-stopping — the format our audience saves and shares.
- **Video:** two house formats — **Compilation Video** (best shots, trendy audio) and **Voiceover Video** (honest review narration).
- Watermark coverage content with the mark-only logo, small, in a corner.

## 6. Tone of Voice

If Eventful were a person: **that friend who always knows what's on this weekend, tells you the truth about it, and is kinda funny about it.**

### Voice sliders
| Trait | We are | We are not |
|---|---|---|
| Honest | Blunt, specific, fair | Mean, snarky for sport |
| Funny | Dry, self-aware, playful | Try-hard, meme-spam, cringe |
| Confident | Backed by numbers | Arrogant, superlative-stuffed |
| Local | SG/MY casual, plain-spoken | Corporate, formal, "esteemed patrons" |
| Helpful | Dates, prices, directions, verdicts | Vague hype with no info |

### The four rules
1. **Say it like a friend, not a flyer.** "This new banh mi spot is worth the queue" beats "Indulge in an unforgettable culinary experience."
2. **Receipts over adjectives.** Numbers, dates and specifics do the persuading: ">500 customers daily", "800% increase", "first 20 sign-ups get in."
3. **Honesty is non-negotiable.** If something's mid, we say so (kindly). Never write a paid post that reads like a lie — the deal is honest coverage, and clients buy us *because* of that.
4. **Underplay, then land the punchline.** Our humor is understatement and self-awareness: "our videos are pretty good. but our statics are even better. and we're kinda funny." Casual aside > exclamation marks.

### Signature moves (use these)
- The **"oh btw"** aside: "Oh btw, we do not run ads. Every single engagement is organic."
- The **lowercase punchline**: "let's sell out your next event :)"
- The **honest verdict** framing: "the good, the great, and the surprising."
- The **stat drop**: lead or close with one big number, not five.
- A single **:)** or well-placed emoji beats a row of 🎉🔥😍.

### Never do this
- ALL-CAPS hype walls: "BIGGEST SALE EVER‼️ ONLY 4 DAYS‼️ SO COME DOWN NOW‼️‼️" — this is the style we exist as an antidote to.
- Corporate filler: "delighted to announce", "look no further", "hidden gem" (retired from overuse), "foodie paradise".
- Fake urgency or fake praise. If we wouldn't tell a friend to go, we don't tell 1.8M people to go.
- Emoji info-block dumps as a substitute for a caption with a point of view.

### Tone by context
| Context | Register | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| IG/TikTok captions | Most casual, punchline-forward | Hook in line 1, verdict early, info block (dates/price/location) at the end, clean and short. |
| Honest reviews | Casual but fair | Praise specifically, criticize specifically. One-liner verdict. |
| Giveaways/community | Warm + clear mechanics | Simple steps, real deadline, no dark patterns. |
| Telegram | Utility-first | Fast, info-dense, minimal jokes — it's a feed people scan. |
| Client decks & emails (B2B) | Confident, warm, still human | Numbers lead. Personality stays ("and we're kinda funny") but mechanics, rates and timelines are crisp and precise: Ideation 2 days → Production 3 days → Vetting 2 days → 1 week total. |
| Malaysia channels | Same voice, local references | Don't copy-paste SG captions; localize slang, places, prices (RM). The voice travels, the references don't. |

### Caption formula (social)
1. **Hook** — one line, specific, no clickbait lies.
2. **Verdict/angle** — what we actually think, in 1–2 casual sentences.
3. **Details** — 📍 location · 🗓 dates · 💸 price. Max one emoji per line, used as bullets not decoration.
4. **CTA** — light: "go before everyone finds out" / "tag the friend who's paying."

### Before / after
- ❌ "BIGGEST FOOD FEST OF THE YEAR!! 100+ STALLS!! DON'T MISS OUT!!!"
  ✅ "100+ stalls, and we tried 14 so you don't gamble your money. here's the shortlist."
- ❌ "We are delighted to partner with Brand X for this exciting activation."
  ✅ "Brand X asked us to come down. we did. honestly? the sample counter alone is worth the trip."
