# INVADE DROPS — concept mockup

A design pitch: streetwear-style **limited-release "drops" applied to festival experiences**, built for [Invade](https://invade.co/) (the team behind Artbox, Eatbox and Shilin Night Market in Singapore/KL).

Open `index.html` in any browser — it's a single self-contained page (Google Fonts is the only external dependency, with system-font fallbacks).

## Who Invade is (research summary)

- **Invade** (Invade Industry Pte Ltd, founded 2010, founder/CEO Kent Teo) is a Singapore-based creative retail activation and events company operating in Singapore and Malaysia, working with a network of ~40,000 brands.
- Two sides of the business:
  1. **invade.co** — a tech-enabled marketplace where merchants, makers and SMEs discover and book retail/pop-up spaces and event stalls in real time.
  2. **Event IPs / festival agency (byinvade.co)** — they own and run some of the region's biggest creative festivals and markets, and offer end-to-end "festivalisation" services (concept, marketing, design, fabrication, execution).
- **Signature event IPs:**
  - **Artbox Singapore** — their flagship creative festival (art × food × retail). Artbox 2026 ("Artbox Camp") runs 3–5 & 10–12 April 2026 at Singapore Expo Hall 3, 12pm–11pm, 250+ brands, 40+ artists, ~$7–8 entry, with a wiggle wiggle (Korea) showcase.
  - **Eatbox** — food-festival spinoff ("local funfair of good food").
  - **Shilin Night Market** — Taiwanese-inspired night market pop-up, one of SG's most anticipated pop-ups.
  - **Makers' Market** — artisan/handmade markets at malls (Suntec, PLQ, Katong).
  - **Fleawhere** — pop-up thrift/flea markets.
  - **MOX** — co-making space for the creative community (near SOTA).

## The concept: why "drops"

Invade's events are **high-volume and broad** (Artbox sees 10,000+ visitors/day). What they don't currently sell is **scarcity** — and their audience (creative, hype-literate, streetwear-adjacent Gen Z/millennials) is exactly the crowd that queues for drops.

**INVADE DROPS** layers a limited-release mechanic on top of their existing IPs — small, numbered, one-night-only experiences that use infrastructure they already have:

| Drop | IP | Run | Idea |
|---|---|---|---|
| **001 · Artbox After Dark** | Artbox | 500 passes | The festival stays open 11pm–2am for 500 pass-holders only: supper menus, artist midnight editions, numbered totes printed on site |
| 002 · Supper Club | Eatbox | 200 seats | 12 hawkers × 12 chefs, one long-table supper, sealed menu |
| 003 · Night Pass | Shilin | 800 passes | First hour before public entry + stamped snack passport |
| 004 · Makers' Edition | Makers' Market | 50 pieces | Fifty one-of-one maker pieces dropped online at once |

**Drop mechanics** (consistent every time, so the brand compounds):
- Fixed drop time — always Friday 20:00 SGT
- SMS waitlist with a 15-minute head start
- Passes numbered in claim order (Nº 001 = fastest hands)
- Zero restocks — a pass is entry + credit + collectible
- Every scanned pass auto-joins the next drop's early-access list (retention loop)

**Why it works for Invade:**
- Near-zero marginal cost — the venue, vendors and crowd already exist; After Dark monetises hours the hall is otherwise dark.
- Scarcity generates the social content (queue posts, pass-number flexes) that feeds ticket sales for the *main* event.
- The numbered pass becomes recurring merch/collectible revenue and a first-party SMS audience they own.
