## 1. The channel reality (and why it changes the plan)

Across all 54 targets, **zero personal executive emails are published anywhere** — FMCG and F&B groups simply don't publish them. What IS available:

| Channel | Coverage | Conversion quality |
|---|---|---|
| Company/marketing inboxes (marketing@, press@, partnerships@) | ~30 of 54 | Medium — gatekept, but marketing@ inboxes at Gigi, Paradise, Katrina, Empire Sushi, Marrybrown explicitly invite collabs |
| Published business WhatsApp lines | 7 (Lo & Behold, ZUS, myBurgerLab, Inside Scoop, Dutch Lady, Nestlé MY text line, Secret Recipe) | High — SEA execs live on WhatsApp; these are *published business* lines, so it's a legitimate first touch |
| LinkedIn DM/connect | ~35 confirmed profiles | Highest for the person-level targets — this is where marketing directors actually reply |
| Pattern-guess emails | 3 usable (Gerald Yeo, Ying-ying Lee, Indra Kantono) | Use only as a parallel test; never claim you "have" their email |

**Recommendation: run a 3-channel sequence, not an email blast.** Email-only to company inboxes will convert ~1-2%. Email + LinkedIn + (where published) WhatsApp, personalized with the pride point + a radar stat, should realistically get 15-25% reply on T1s.

## 2. Eventful's unfair advantage — lead with the radar

Every agency cold-emails "we make viral videos." Nobody else can open with **the prospect's own live performance data**. The radar (results.json / results-sg.json) measures likes/hr velocity and outperformance multipliers across SG & KL food/lifestyle pages.

The hook that converts is a **1-line free insight**, e.g.:
- "Your Kuromi takeover post did 3.1× your usual velocity — here's the 48h window you left on the table."
- "kl.foodie's post about your promo hit 1,340 likes/hr this week — top 0.1% in KL. Your own page captured none of that traffic."

**Before each send, pull one real stat for that brand (or its category) from the radar. If the radar hasn't scanned that brand, use the category benchmark.** Never invent a number.

## 3. The email itself (anatomy that converts)

- **Subject (≤7 words):** their brand + a number or their win. "Jigger & Pony at No. 9 — one idea" / "Kuromi did 3× — next IP window"
- **Line 1 — pride point:** congratulate the specific thing (from contacts.csv). One sentence, no flattery adjectives.
- **Line 2 — radar insight:** one real number about them or their category.
- **Line 3 — offer:** "We're @eventfulsg — we make short-form food content engineered from this data. One idea for <brand>: <one-line concept>."
- **CTA:** "Worth 15 minutes? I'll bring the full <brand> radar readout either way."
- **≤120 words. No deck. No links except @eventfulsg.** First-person, from a named human at Eventful.
- Gatekept inboxes (press@/feedback@): add "Could you forward this to <Name>, <Title>? It's specific to their work on <campaign>."

## 4. Sequence & timing

| Day | Touch |
|---|---|
| 0 | Email 1 (pride point + radar stat) — Tue/Wed/Thu, 08:30–10:00 local |
| 2 | LinkedIn connect, 1-line note referencing the email ("sent a <brand> radar readout to your team inbox — the 3× stat is real") |
| 6 | Email 2 — a NEW radar data point ("since I wrote, your competitor X posted and did Y") |
| 12 | Breakup email — attach the free readout anyway ("no reply needed — keeping this useful") |
| — | WhatsApp (published business lines only): single polite message, only if no email/LinkedIn response, or as first touch for the 7 brands where it's the *published* channel |

Batch discipline: send T1s first (~20 contacts), 5-8/day, iterate subject lines on reply data before touching T2/T3. Warm up the sending domain; use hello@ on your own domain, not the personal Gmail, and keep volume <30/day to stay out of spam.
