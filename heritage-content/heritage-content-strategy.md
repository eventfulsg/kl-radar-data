## 1. Your tone of voice

- **Simple, spoken Singapore English.** Short declaratives, contractions, zero jargon. "Their income disappeared, and they needed money." Local anchors used casually: NS, pasar malam, hawker centre, NTUC, Woodleigh Mall, Serangoon.
- **Narrator voice = calm documentary, not hype.** You never say "insane/crazy/must-read". The drama comes from facts and numbers, delivered flat: "An employee stole $30,000."
- **Numbers are the emotion.** Every act break is a number: S$50–60k/month, 45%→20%, $15k→$10k→$5k losses, 12 recipes, $250K, 54M subscribers. Digits, never spelled out.
- **Staccato for emphasis.** "Every. Single. Day." / "No algorithm. No mentor. No shortcut." / "Non-negotiable."
- **Soft-censored edge**: dr*g offence — keeps it IG-safe while signalling honesty.
- **Second person only twice**: pulling the reader in at the top ("You walk into IKEA, just to 'look around'") and the CTA at the end ("Follow us if you're an entrepreneur in Singapore").
- **Every photo credited** ("cr. …") — quiet authority + repost safety.

## 2. Hook anatomy (cover slide)

Formula: `This Is How/Why + [known name] + [unbelievable but specific outcome] (+ timeframe)`
- Outcome must contain a number or a paradox: "12 Stores In 3 Years", "Even Though You Don't Buy Anything", "Nearly Killed Its Own Brand", "In The Name Of His Son".
- 3 lines max, centered, Title Case, huge white bold rounded sans; ONE phrase can take the green accent (you did this with "Shopping Mall").
- Above headline: navy HERITAGE badge. Below: "READ MORE ⊙" in letter-spaced serif caps — the swipe trigger.
- Cover photo: real, candid, human — founder laughing at his stall, storefront neon, street shot. Never stock-posed.

## 3. Retention system (why people swipe to the end)

1. **One thought per slide, 1–3 short lines.** The story is rationed; the thumb has to keep moving.
2. **Sentences split across slides mid-thought**: "But somehow what felt like minutes," → next slide → "Turned into hours." A micro-cliffhanger every 2 seconds.
3. **Ellipsis + payoff pairs**: "they believed in one thing..." → "That their matcha would outlast the hype."
4. **The '1 decision' beat**: "And they made 1 decision" / "He made 1 decision." — appears in 3 of 7 posts. It's your signature mid-story hook; keep it.
5. **Rise → peak → collapse**: good news slides ("Everything seemed great") exist only to set up "But that was when everything went downhill."
6. **A quoted emotional beat at the low point**: "I felt hopeless and alone." / "Will history repeat itself?"
7. **Recovery in exactly 3 moves**: Firstly / Next / Lastly (JIN) — concrete, numbered, teachable.
8. **Branded segment headers near the end**: The Standardization / The Journey / The Food / The Malls / The Result / The Real Win / The Lesson — "The [X]" with X accented. This converts a story into business advice (your niche promise).
9. **The Lesson generalizes to the reader's own business** — one transferable principle, never a summary.
10. **Fixed CTA slide**: "This is Heritage, and we'll be posting business advice from the Top 1% in Singapore / Follow us (only) if you're an entrepreneur in Singapore." The "only if" filter is doing positioning work — keep it.

## 4. Design system

- **Canvas**: 1080×1350 (4:5). Full-bleed photograph on every slide — no flat-colour slides, ever. Story = photos; brand = overlay.
- **Overlay**: dark gradient (bottom-heavy on covers; whole-frame dim on text-heavy slides) so white text always passes contrast.
- **Type**: bold rounded geometric sans for all narrative text (white, centered); serif reserved for brand elements (HERITAGE wordmark, READ MORE) — the serif/sans split reads "premium brand, casual storyteller".
- **Colours**: navy #1C3F7F (badge/logo), white, green accent for the money phrase, tiny grey credit line. Nothing else.
- **Fixed furniture on every slide**: centered semi-transparent HERITAGE watermark above the text block + "cr." credit. Text block sits mid-to-low, never covering faces.
- **Photos**: candid founder shots > storefronts > press photos (ST, Reuters, Zaobao, Mothership credited) > brand's own IG. Faces and real places, minimal graphics.
- **Emerging variant** (Lau Wang latest copy): slide 1 = Joston personal intro ("Hi, my name's Joston, and I am the founder of Eventful.") before the story — personal-brand bridge. Worth A/B testing on the next 2 posts.
