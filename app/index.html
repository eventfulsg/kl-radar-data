✍️ Caption cheat sheet (house style)

HARD RULE — every food-deal caption has a number

Price (RM78 nett), date (27 Feb), discount (80% OFF) or scarcity count (only 4 days). No number, no post.

Mode 1 · Meme / nostalgia

One casual line, lowercase texting voice ("u", "btw"), POV framing. Credit reposts: Video credit: @handle. 1–2 hashtags.

Mode 2 · Food deal / event (bread & butter)

Conversational hook with dramatic ellipses ("I know… again….. but it's finally back"), then emoji info block one fact per line: 🗓️ dates · ⏰ times · 📍 venue · 🚇 nearest LRT/MRT · 🚗 parking. Asterisk fine print (*T&Cs apply, while stocks last). Casual CTA to sister page. 3–5 hashtags: #malaysia #klfoodie + niche.

Mode 3 · Big sale hype

🚨 ALL-CAPS hook with superlative + scarcity + number ("BIGGEST … ONLY 4 DAYS … UP TO 80% OFF"). Short hype paras, name-drop brands, full emoji info block, close in caps: "SO COME DOWN NOW‼️‼️"

Ranking

Fresh posts (≤48h) ranked by ⚡ outperformance — how far a post is above the posting page's own usual numbers (median engagement of that page's older posts, adjusted for how young the post is so new posts get a fair shot). "3.4× their usual" means this post already has 3.4 times the engagement a normal post from that page would have at the same age. Velocity breaks ties: 📈 likes gained per hour between the two morning scans, or ⏱ lifetime likes/hour on first sighting. Collab posts across the Foodie network are deduped and tagged 🤝. The orange bar on each post shows outperformance at a glance — the dark tick marks 1× (that page's normal); the bar maxes out at 6×.

Why outperformance

A big page's routine post always has big totals — that's not a trend, that's Tuesday. A smaller page suddenly doing 4× its usual numbers means the CONTENT is what's blowing up, so we can cover it early. Orange badge = 2× or more, solid orange = 4× or more.
